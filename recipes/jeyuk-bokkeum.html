Jeyuk Bokkeum (제육볶음)

Description

Jeyuk bokkeum is spicy marinated pork, also called dweji bulgogi (돼지불고기) or pork bulgogi. The marinade is a fragrant mixture of ginger, garlic, and gochujang (seasoned fermented red chili paste).

It can be served in a variety of ways. As a filling for lettuce wraps, a topping for rice bowls, or by itself with side dishes. The combination of sweet and spicy flavors pairs well with many other side dishes.

Slicing the meat thinly speeds up both the marinating and grilling processes, and maximizes the amount of char-grilled flavor you get (more surface area).


Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork belly or shoulder
  • 1/2 medium onion
  • 3 scallions
  • red or green leaf lettuce, for serving

Marinade

  • 6 tablespoons gochujang
  • 1 tablespoon korean red chili flakes (gochugaru)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons rice wine or mirin
  • 3 tablespoons sugar or brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 cloves fresh garlic
  • 1 tablespoon fresh grated ginger
  • 1/2 apple, grated

Steps

  1. Slice the pork and onions thinly and cut the scallions into bite-size pieces (about 2 inches).
  2. Combine marinade ingredients in large resealable plastic bag. Add sliced meat, scallions, and onions. Toss to coat evenly.

    NOTE: Marinating in a plastic bag makes it easier to toss the ingredients for even coverage of the marinade.

  3. Let rest for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Transfer marinated ingredients into a bowl and set aside.
  5. Heat oil in a frying pan.
  6. Stir fry the marinated ingredients in 2 batches over medium-high heat until caramelized.

    NOTE: Do not overfill the pan. Too many ingredients in the pan will cause the meat to take longer to cook and the outside will burn before it is cooked through.

  7. Remove from heat, garnish with toasted sesame seeds, and serve over lettuce.
