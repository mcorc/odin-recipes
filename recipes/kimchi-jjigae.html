Kimchi Jjigae (김치찌개)

Description

Kimchi jjigae is stew made with old, sour kimchi, a classic Korean comfort food. It is often made with fatty pork (belly or ribs), which pairs well with the strongly acidic and spicy kimchi. The stew is rounded out with tofu and scallions to add body, texture, and flavor.

For the broth, traditionally the water from rinsing rice is used to thicken the stew, as it contains starch. You can use chicken, vegetable, or anchovy broth for a stronger flavor. The juice from the kimchi is an important component of the broth. If not using kimchi juice, add more salt to compensate.


Ingredients

  • 2 cups kimchi, fully fermented
  • 1/2 cup juice from kimchi
  • 2 cups water or broth (chicken, vegetable, or anchovy)
  • 4 ounches pork belly (or other fatty pork)
  • 6 ounces tofu
  • 2 scallions
  • 1 teaspoon red chili flakes
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Cut kimchi, pork, and scallions into bite-size pieces. Slice tofu about 1/2 inch thick.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a small pot (table size, not a large soup pot).
  3. Add kimchi, pork, red pepper flakes, and garlic. Cook over medium-high heat 5-7 minutes, until kimchi is soft and pork is cooked through.
  4. Add kimchi juice and broth and bring to a boil. Boil for 5 minutes.
  5. Reduce heat to medium, cover, and cook for about 15 minutes.
  6. Add tofu and scallions and cook 5 minutes or until tofu is cooked through.
  7. Taste and add salt and pepper as needed.
  8. Serve immediately, while bubbling.
